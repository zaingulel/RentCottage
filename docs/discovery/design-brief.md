# RentCottage design exploration brief

This is a visual prototype, not production UI or a final product specification. It explores three ways to present the same Arabic-first, web-first cottage marketplace to a client before implementation begins.

## The question

Which visual and information-design direction best communicates a trustworthy, simple way to discover and request a cottage stay in Iraq?

## Shared product assumptions

- The MVP is a controlled marketplace of manually approved cottage owners.
- Customers browse without an account and verify a phone number only when submitting a request.
- A stay can cover one or more consecutive nights. A cottage owner accepts or declines within four hours.
- Search considers location, dates, number of nights, guest capacity and key amenities.
- A customer sees only approximate location before confirmation. Exact directions and mutual contact details appear after an owner accepts.
- Arabic and RTL are launch requirements. The customer surface is mobile-friendly web, not a native app.

## Variants

### A — The Retreat

An emotional, image-led landing experience. It starts with the feeling of escaping to a cottage, then introduces search as a calm, focused action. Best if the client wants a premium, aspirational brand that sells the experience first.

### B — The Planner

A practical search workspace with clear filters and a dense availability-led result list. Best if the client wants customers to compare options quickly and values confidence and efficiency over lifestyle marketing.

### C — Trusted Stays

A guided, reassurance-first flow that explains the request-and-accept model, publication checks and the information released after confirmation. Best if the client expects trust, new-market education and operational clarity to be the main adoption barriers.

## Review guidance

Ask the client to pick a primary direction, then name specific elements to keep from the others. For example: “the image treatment from A, the filters from B, and the reassurance steps from C.” This is more useful than choosing a colour palette in isolation.

## Still open

The prototype deliberately avoids making claims about payments, refunds, cancellation terms, owner-document storage, launch geography, exact check-in/out times, booking cut-off, and support/dispute responsibility. Those remain in [the client-question log](./client-questions.md).

## Prototype verdict

_Not chosen yet. Capture the selected direction and the reasons here, then delete the losing variants before production work begins._
